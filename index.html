Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Most helmets have a recommended lifespan of about 3 to 5 years, depending on the materials and usage. If your helmet is older than this, it's a good idea to consider a replacement, even if it appears to be in good condition.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Check the tire pressure. Tires often lose air while sitting, so inflate them to the recommended pressure (usually marked on the sidewall). Look for cracks, dry rot, or flat spots. If the tires are dry or brittle, they may need replacing.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Ride with Traffic: Always ride in the same direction as motor vehicles. Follow the flow of traffic, not against it. Traffic Signals and Signs: Stop at all stop signs, red lights, and obey other traffic signals just like a car would. Yield to Pedestrians: Always give right of way to pedestrians at crosswalks.
